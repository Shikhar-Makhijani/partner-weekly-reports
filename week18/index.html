Recent Partner Challenges and Asks (refer broader tracker here)

Signals surfaced this week from EMEA partner leadership conversations (via Sunil). Core theme: partners want to operate more independently, predictably, and with confidence in both the platform and the commercial model.

Platform confidence on Partner-internal use — Partners using DevRev internally (our strongest enablement lever) are surfacing reliability issues on Computer and certain connectors; in at least one case (RevEdge) activity is currently paused pending trust-rebuild. Given RevEdge is one of our most credible CX-focused EMEA partners, this is a high-impact signal. Tight feedback loops with the product + eng teams on internal-use incidents will help us rebuild conviction and protect advocacy potential.

NFR (Not-For-Resale) Program for Partner internal adoption — EMEA partners are explicitly asking for a structured NFR program that gives them a low-friction, production-like environment for internal use (clear provisioning, supported integrations, prioritized support). A well-executed NFR path accelerates hands-on learning, hardens platform confidence and converts internal use into credible customer-facing advocacy.

GSI readiness — enterprise integrations — With strategic conversations advancing at Capgemini, Accenture, EY and similar, GSIs expect enterprise-grade integrations (SAP, Oracle NetSuite, etc.) to exist or be strongly supported by DevRev rather than co-built. A small dedicated AAI motion + team around these enterprise systems would materially increase GSI traction and speed up time-to-pipeline.

Pricing & commercial autonomy for partners — Partners are asking for more transparency and the ability to operate more independently in-market: local-currency pricebooks (EUR/GBP), clear pricing guardrails they can take to customers, and involvement (via a small advisory group) in pricing discussions. This is about enabling predictable customer conversations without needing per-deal escalations back to HQ.
